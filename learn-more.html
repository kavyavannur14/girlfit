What is Menstruation?

The menstrual cycle is a complex, natural process that occurs in the female reproductive system, preparing the body for a potential pregnancy. It is regulated by a finely tuned interplay of hormones and typically spans around 28 days, though it can range from 21 to 35 days in different individuals. The cycle includes several phases: the menstrual phase, follicular phase, ovulation, and luteal phase ‚Äî each playing a distinct role in reproductive health.

The Phases of the Menstrual Cycle:

1. Menstrual Phase

This is the first stage, marked by the shedding of the uterine lining, which exits the body through the vagina. This bleeding, commonly referred to as a "period," lasts for 3 to 7 days and involves a mix of blood, mucus, and tissue.

2. Follicular Phase

Starting on the first day of menstruation and ending with ovulation, this phase involves the maturation of follicles in the ovaries under the influence of follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH). One dominant follicle will prepare to release an egg.

3. Ovulation

Around mid-cycle, a surge in luteinizing hormone (LH) triggers the release of a mature egg from the ovary. This is the most fertile period of the cycle, and fertilization is most likely if sperm are present in the reproductive tract.

4. Luteal Phase

After ovulation, the ruptured follicle becomes the corpus luteum and secretes progesterone to maintain the uterine lining. If fertilization doesn't occur, hormone levels drop, leading to the next menstrual period.

Up to this point, we've explored how periods are a natural and essential part of growing up. But sometimes, things don't go as expected. There's another side ‚Äî where periods become irregular, painful, or stop showing up entirely. Recognizing the symptoms early can make a big difference. Let's understand PCOD, PCOS and how they can affect girls and women ‚Äî physically and emotionally.

üíñ Period Care Tips & Check-in Questions

How can I support her emotionally?
Listen without judgment, offer kindness, and remind her that it's okay to rest and feel moody.
What comfort items help during cramps?
A heating pad, cozy blanket, hot water, or chocolate can be comforting during painful days.
How do I check if she's feeling healthy?
Ask gently: "How many days is your period?", "Any dizziness or fatigue?", "Was your cycle on time?"
üåø What kind of food helps during periods?
Offer iron-rich foods like spinach, bananas, dark chocolate, or warm soups. Avoid junk or overly salty food.
